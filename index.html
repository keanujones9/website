Our Solar System

There are many planetary systems like ours in the universe, with planets orbiting a host star.
Our planetary system is called “the solar system” because we use the word “solar” to describe
things related to our star, after the Latin word for Sun, "solis."

Here are some must know things as well as facts about the Solar System:
Our solar system is made up of a star, eight planets, and countless smaller bodies such as dwarf planets, asteroids, and comets.
Our solar system orbits the center of the Milky Way galaxy at about 515,000 mph (828,000 kph). We’re in one of the galaxy’s four spiral arms.
Our solar system is the only one known to support life. So far, we only know of life on Earth, but NASA is looking for more.
Out of all the planets and some asteroids in our Solar System combined, hold more than 200 moons within their orbits!
Our Solar System is a region of space within the Milky Way galaxy which their is estimated to be over 100 billion planets.
